### Paediatric First Aid (Detailed)

#### What It Covers

**Infant-specific skills (newbornâ€“12 months):**
- **Infant CPR** â€“ one-handed chest compressions (using 2 fingers or heel of hand), reduced compression depth
- **Infant airway positioning** â€“ keeping neutral to prevent obstruction (very different from adult head-tilt)
- **Choking in infants** â€“ back blows, chest thrusts (never abdominal thrusts, which can rupture organs)
- **SIDS risk awareness** â€“ sleep position, temperature, feeding recognition

**Child-specific skills (1â€“16 years):**
- **Child CPR** â€“ hand position variations by age, appropriate compression depth
- **Child choking** â€“ back blows, abdominal thrusts (now safe, unlike infants)
- **Febrile convulsions** â€“ recognising fever-triggered seizures, management
- **Asthma and allergies** â€“ recognising attacks, positioning, when to use inhalers/EpiPens
- **Poisoning/ingestion** â€“ recognising common household toxins, first response
- **Fractures and sprains** â€“ appropriate immobilisation for children
- **Paediatric shock** â€“ managing shock in children (different vital signs than adults)

#### Why Paediatric Is Different

The human body changes dramatically birth to adulthood. CPR techniques, airway anatomy, medication dosesâ€”everything varies:

| Factor | Infant | Child (1â€“12) | Teenager (13+) |
|--------|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Airway anatomy** | Narrowest at cricoid ring | Funnel-shaped larynx | Adult-like by 12 |

| **Compression depth** | ~4cm | ~5cm | ~5â€“6cm |
| **Breathing rate** | 40â€“60 breaths/min | 20â€“30 breaths/min | 12â€“20 breaths/min |
| **Heart rate** | 100â€“160 bpm | 70â€“140 bpm | 60â€“100 bpm |
| **AED use** | Attenuated (reduced) pads | Child-sized pads | Adult pads |

**Why this matters:** If you use adult CPR technique on an infant, you risk rib fractures, internal injuries, or airway damage. Paediatric training teaches age-appropriate responses.

#### CQC Requirement

**For childcare/early years:**
- **OFSTED requires** Paediatric First Aid (Level 3) for all staff
- Specifically: HSE-approved, one-day course
- **Validity:** 3 years (some settings require refreshers every 2 years for added safety)
- **Non-compliance:** If staff lack current Paediatric First Aid, OFSTED can rate you Inadequate on Safety grounds

**For other settings:** If your service involves contact with children (e.g., community support, family visiting), Paediatric training is strongly recommended.

### Common Questions

**Q: Can one course cover both adults and children?**  
A: No. Adult CPR on a child can cause injury. Children's CPR differs anatomically. You need both if your role spans ages.

**Q: Do I need annual refreshers?**  
A: Certificates are valid 3 years. However, many care providers do annual refreshers because First Aid skills degrade. CQC isn't strict about this, but it shows commitment to safety.

**Q: What if I'm trained in Adult CPR but need to help a child?**  
A: In an emergency, do what you've been trained for. But ideally, you should have Paediatric training *before* a crisis. That's why both courses exist.

**Q: Is online First Aid training acceptable?**  
A: No. CQC and HSE require in-person, hands-on practice. Online courses don't teach physical skills. This is non-negotiable.

### Real-World Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Care assistant discovers a bruise on a resident**

**With Level 1 only:**  
- Recognises it might be a sign of abuse
- Reports to team leader
- Doesn't know what happens next

**With Level 2 (team leader):**  
- Receives the report
- Knows to document observations (size, shape, location, resident statement)
- Contacts local authority safeguarding team
- Supports the care assistant and resident
- Keeps appropriate records
- Understands investigation timeline

**With Level 3 (registered manager):**  
- Oversees entire process
- Manages staff allegations (if relevant)
- Ensures policy is followed
- Liaises with multi-agency team
- Reviews care plans for risk
- Reports to board/regulators
- Evaluates organizational safeguarding systems

Each level adds responsibility and depth.
